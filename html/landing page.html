What Are You Recycling Today?

Recyclables

Recyclables include materials like plastics, paper, cardboard, glass, and metals (such as aluminum cans or steel).

E-Waste

E-waste, or electronic waste, refers to discarded electronic devices like phones, computers, TVs, batteries, and appliances.

Textile Waste

Textile waste includes discarded clothing, fabrics, and other materials like bedding or towels.

Recycling Tips

1. Know Your Local Guidelines

Understand what materials are accepted in your local recycling program to avoid contamination.

2. Clean and Rinse

Rinse food containers to prevent contamination and ensure the recyclables are processed properly.

3. Avoid Plastic Bags

Never put plastic bags in your recycling bin. Take them to designated drop-off locations at stores.

4. Separate Materials

Follow local rules on separating recyclables like paper, plastic, and glass to improve recycling efficiency.

5. Flatten Cardboard

Break down and flatten cardboard boxes to save space and make processing easier.

6. Recycle E-Waste Properly

Take electronics like batteries and phones to specialized e-waste recycling centers.

7. Don't Recycle Food-Soiled Items

Avoid putting greasy pizza boxes or dirty paper in the recycling bin—they belong in the compost or trash.

8. Remove Bottle Caps

Take off bottle caps before recycling, as they’re often made from different materials that need separate processing.

9. Buy Recycled Products

Support recycling by purchasing products made from recycled materials, closing the recycling loop.

10. Donate or Repurpose

Donate gently used items or repurpose them before recycling to reduce waste.
